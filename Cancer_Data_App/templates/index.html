What is cancer?

Cancer is a name given to a collection of related diseases. The main commonality is that some of the body's cells begin to divide without stopping and spread into surrounding tissues.

How many cancer types exist?

Over 120 cancer types have been found, each characterized by abnormal cell growth. Nevertheless, many are treatable with a high survival rate.

What are the deadliest types?

  1. Lung and bronchail cancer
  2. Colon and rectal cancer
  3. Breast cancer
  4. Pancreatic cancer
  5. Prostate cancer
  6. Leukemia
  7. Non-Hodgkin lymphoma
  8. Liver and intrahepatic bile duct cancer
  9. Ovarian cancer
  10. Esophageal cancer
